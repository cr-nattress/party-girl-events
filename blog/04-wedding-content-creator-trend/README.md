# The wedding content creator trend (same-day Reels/TikTok)

## Purpose of this brief
This README is an internal “blog post brief” for explaining what wedding content creators do, how they differ from traditional photo/video, and how to hire one.

## Why this topic is trending (signals to reference)
- **Content creators are a named wedding tech trend**: The Knot lists “Wedding Content Creators” as a 2025 wedding technology trend and describes demand for quick-turn reels/stories (same-day or 1–2 days).
  - Source: https://www.theknot.com/content/wedding-technology-trends
- **Social media influences wedding decisions**: Zola reports 87% of couples have made wedding planning choices based on social media.
  - Source: https://www.zola.com/expert-advice/the-first-look-report-2026

## Audience + intent
- **Primary reader**
  - Couples who want a social-forward documentation plan.
  - Couples comparing photo/video packages and add-ons.
- **Search intent**
  - “wedding content creator”
  - “same day wedding reels”
  - “behind the scenes wedding content”
  - “wedding content creator vs videographer”

## Angle / positioning
Position content creation as “fast, candid, phone-native storytelling” that complements (not replaces) pro photo/video.

- H3: Typical deliverables

  - same-day recap

### H2: Content creator vs photographer vs videographer
- H3: What each one is best at

### H2: What to ask before hiring a wedding content creator
- H3: Portfolio review (do they match your vibe?)
- H3: Turnaround times and how delivery works
- H3: Boundaries and etiquette

  - when/where filming is not allowed

### H2: The shot list that creates a great recap
- H3: Getting ready
- H3: First look / ceremony beats
- H3: Detail b-roll

### H2: How to integrate content into your full documentation plan

### H2: Pricing variables to explain (keep general)

### H2: Common mistakes
- Hiring a content creator but not giving them access/time to capture key beats
- No plan for who holds phones during key moments
- Overproducing so the day feels staged

### H2: FAQ

- “Can we do an unplugged ceremony and still get content?”
