# Micro weddings & “minimonies”

## Purpose of this brief
This README is an internal “blog post brief” you can use to write a full article (and/or a service page) about micro weddings and “minimonies.”

## Why this topic is trending (signals to reference)
- **Guest experience is a top priority**: The Knot cites that guest experience is a major focus for couples (use this as the bridge to “small guest count, high touch”).
  - Source: https://www.theknot.com/content/wedding-venue-trends
- **Weekend-style celebrations are growing**: Zola reports 37% host at least one additional event and 18% opt for a 2–3 day weekend.
  - Source: https://www.zola.com/expert-advice/the-first-look-report-2026
- **Personalized, flexible timelines are “in”**: The Knot calls out “deconstructed” wedding timelines and non-traditional flows.
  - Source: https://www.theknot.com/content/new-wedding-trends

## Audience + intent
- **Primary reader**
  - Couples considering 10–50 guests.
  - Couples who want a premium experience without a 150-person budget.
- **Search intent**
  - “micro wedding ideas”
  - “minimony package”
  - “small wedding timeline”
  - “intimate wedding venue”

Position micro weddings as “intentional + elevated,” not “scaled down.” Focus on guest care, flow, and high-touch details.
