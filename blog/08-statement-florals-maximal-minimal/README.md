# Statement florals (maximal installs + modern minimal)

## Purpose of this brief
This README is an internal “blog post brief” for a floral design post: how to get a “wow” floral moment (maximal or minimal) without wasting money or flowers.

## Why this topic is trending (signals to reference)
- **Organic, natural florals are highlighted as a trend**: The Knot’s 2026 trends include “Meadowcore narratives” and note looser, more natural arrangements.
  - Source: https://www.theknot.com/content/new-wedding-trends
- **Social media drives floral decisions**: Zola notes couples change palettes and spend more on florals/décor due to social inspiration.
  - Source: https://www.zola.com/expert-advice/the-first-look-report-2026

## Audience + intent
- **Primary reader**
  - Couples choosing between statement installs vs minimalist, sculptural florals.
  - Couples trying to stretch a floral budget.
- **Search intent**
  - “statement wedding flowers”
  - “wedding floral installation cost”
  - “minimalist wedding flowers”
  - “ceremony backdrop flowers”

## Working title ideas
- Statement Wedding Flowers: Where to Spend for Maximum Impact
- Floral Installations vs Minimalist Florals: Which One Looks Better on Camera?

## Angle / positioning
Teach couples to pick one “hero moment,” understand cost drivers (labor/mechanics/seasonality), and plan reuse to maximize impact.

## Suggested structure (H2/H3 outline)
### H2: Two directions trending: maximal installs vs modern minimal
- H3: Maximal: arches, hanging installs, aisle meadows
- H3: Minimal: sculptural pieces, single-variety, negative space

### H2: Where to place the wow (best ROI locations)
- H3: Ceremony backdrop (highest photo density)
- H3: Aisle / entrance moment
- H3: Head table / sweetheart
- H3: Bar moment (guest interaction)

### H2: Floral budget 101: what actually drives cost
- H3: Labor (set-up, strike, ladders, teams)
- H3: Mechanics (structures, foam-free techniques, rentals)
- H3: Seasonality and availability
- H3: Transportation and venue constraints

### H2: How to make your florals look expensive (even if they aren’t)
- Prioritize scale in one area
- Use lighting to amplify florals
- Choose intentional color palettes

### H2: Reuse plans (the “move list”)
- Ceremony flowers → reception
- Aisle markers → centerpieces
- Welcome arrangement → bar

### H2: Common mistakes
- Spending evenly across everything and ending up with nothing impactful
- Ignoring labor/mechanics in quotes
- Choosing flowers that won’t survive heat/wind

### H2: FAQ
- “How much do floral installations cost?”
- “Are foam-free designs more expensive?”
- “Can we reuse ceremony flowers?”
- “What’s the best way to save on florals?”
