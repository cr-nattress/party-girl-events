# Sustainable / eco-friendly weddings

## Purpose of this brief
This README is an internal “blog post brief” for a practical sustainability guide: how to host a beautiful wedding while reducing waste and making values-aligned choices.

## Why this topic is trending (signals to reference)
- **Sustainable venues are explicitly trending**: The Knot calls out “sustainable wedding venues” and highlights eco-minded practices (e.g., reusable water bottles, venues growing/harvesting produce, conscious guest experience).
  - Source: https://www.theknot.com/content/wedding-venue-trends
- **Values-led planning remains central**: Zola frames “shared values” as core to modern weddings.
  - Source: https://www.zola.com/expert-advice/the-first-look-report-2026

## Audience + intent
- **Primary reader**
  - Couples who want sustainability without sacrificing style.
  - Couples who need “what to do” steps, not guilt.
- **Search intent**
  - “sustainable wedding ideas”
  - “eco friendly wedding checklist”
  - “zero waste wedding”
  - “ethical wedding vendors”

## Angle / positioning
Emphasize “high-impact, low-stress” changes and vendor questions that lead to real outcomes.

## Suggested structure (H2/H3 outline)
### H2: What makes a wedding “sustainable” (in real life)
- H3: Define the priorities (waste, carbon, labor ethics, local economy)
- H3: Choose 3–5 sustainability goals you can actually execute

### H2: The biggest-impact changes (do these first)
- H3: Venue + rentals (reduce single-use)
- H3: Food + beverage (waste prevention, donation plans)
- H3: Florals (seasonality, mechanics, reuse, donation)

### H2: Sustainable venue checklist (questions to ask)
- H3: Waste and composting
- H3: Water service (avoid single-use bottles)
- H3: Energy practices
- H3: Vendor flexibility (can you bring your own rentals / caterer?)

### H2: Design and decor that’s beautiful and low-waste
- H3: Rentals vs purchases
- H3: Signage alternatives
- H3: Candles/lighting rules and safe options

### H2: Attire and beauty (a sustainability-friendly approach)
- H3: Rewear/alteration strategy
- H3: Secondhand/vintage options
- H3: Hair/makeup trial plan to avoid product waste

### H2: Invitations + stationery
- H3: Digital invites and wedding websites
- H3: If printing: paper types, quantities, and what to skip

### H2: Favors (what guests actually keep)
- H3: Consumables
- H3: Donation-in-lieu
- H3: On-site personalization (only if it replaces waste)

### H2: Common mistakes
- Greenwashing with lots of small “eco” items that still end up in the trash
- Overbuying decor
- Skipping the waste plan (trash/compost staffing and signage)

### H2: FAQ
- “Is a sustainable wedding more expensive?”
- “What are the easiest swaps?”
- “How do we handle guests who don’t care?”
- “What do we do with flowers after?”
